Goals

In this course, students will

  • learn the mechanics of a simple computational environment
  • engage with empirical approaches to posing and answering questions
  • become conversant in basic concepts of machine learning
  • apply computational methods to practical tasks in their particular studies
  • discuss methodological and field-specific barriers to interdisciplinary research

Synopsis

This course prepares students in the humanities to engage with computational methods and mindsets.

Grading

Each student will present and lead discussion of a paper chosen with instructor approval, complete one short writing assignment, and employ a computational method to test an empirical hypothesis relevant to their research. Active participation is expected in both discussions and applied, hands-on workshops.
